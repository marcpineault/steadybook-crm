# Calm Money Content Playbook

## Purpose

Generate lead-driving content for Marc Pereira's financial planning practice at Co-operators in London, Ontario. Every piece of content exists to move the right people from scrolling to reaching out.

## Target Audience

- **Primary**: Professionals earning 70k+ with 100k+ saved, living in southwestern Ontario
- **Secondary**: Business owners (incorporated or not) unsure if they're optimizing tax/retirement/insurance
- **Psychographic**: Doing well on paper but quietly uncertain — "Am I on track?" "Am I missing something?" "Should I be doing more?"

They're not looking for a financial advisor. They need to feel seen first, then trust you, then act.

## Content Generation Workflow

1. **Research first** — Use `/last30days` to find what's trending: financial topics people are discussing, viral formats, platform changes, news that affects your audience
2. **Pick topics** from the topic bank below or surface new ones from research
3. **Generate scripts** in the right format (short/long/written)
4. **Review and film**

## The Three Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: "Is This You?" (Short Form — 30-60 sec)

**Platforms**: Instagram Reels, TikTok, LinkedIn Shorts, YouTube Shorts

**Goal**: Pattern-interrupt. Stop the scroll by describing your audience's exact situation. They think "how does this guy know my life?" and watch to the end.

**Structure**:
```
HOOK (0-3 sec): Bold statement or question that calls out the viewer
PAIN (3-15 sec): Describe their situation so accurately they feel exposed
INSIGHT (15-40 sec): One actionable idea they haven't considered
CTA (40-60 sec): Comment trigger or soft ask
```

**Hook formulas that work**:
- "You make [income] and still feel broke. Here's why."
- "If you have [amount] saved and no plan, watch this."
- "Business owners in Ontario — you're probably overpaying tax by [X]. Let me explain."
- "Nobody told you this about [RRSP/TFSA/disability insurance/group benefits]."
- "You're [age range] with a family and no life insurance. Here's what that actually means."
- "Stop putting money into your RRSP if [specific situation]."
- "The #1 mistake I see from [audience segment]."
- "I'm a financial advisor in London, Ontario. Here's what I wish my clients knew sooner."

**CTA options**:
- "Comment PLAN and I'll send you a link to my free retirement quiz" → calmmoney.ca/quiz
- "Comment READY and I'll share my free [resource name]"
- "Link in bio to book a free 15-minute call — no pitch, just answers"
- "Save this for later. Follow for more."

### Pillar 2: "Here's What Nobody Tells You" (Long Form — 3-10 min)

**Platforms**: YouTube, LinkedIn video, repurpose as podcast clips

**Goal**: Deep trust. Position Marc as the expert who gives real, specific, useful answers to things people are quietly Googling. These are the videos that make someone say "I need to talk to this guy."

**Structure**:
```
HOOK (0-15 sec): "Most people get this wrong" or question from a real scenario
CONTEXT (15-60 sec): Why this matters, who this applies to
MEAT (1-7 min): Walk through the actual answer — specific numbers, examples, Ontario-specific details
TAKEAWAY (30 sec): One clear action step
CTA (15 sec): Book a call or take the quiz
```

**Topic approach**: Take something your audience is Googling and answer it better, more specifically, and more personally than any article they'll find. Ontario-specific, income-specific, situation-specific.

### Pillar 3: "The Calm Money Take" (Written — LinkedIn/Facebook/Instagram)

**Platforms**: LinkedIn posts, Facebook, Instagram captions

**Goal**: Stay top of mind. Build authority through consistent, opinionated, warm takes on financial topics. These compound over time — someone sees 10 of your posts over 2 months and then books when the moment is right.

**Structure**:
```
HOOK LINE: One punchy sentence or bold claim
BODY (3-8 sentences): Your take — warm, plain language, no jargon, locally rooted
CTA: Soft — question to the audience, or "quiz link in comments"
```

**Voice guidelines**:
- Talk like you're explaining to a friend at a barbecue
- Confident but never salesy
- Use "you" not "clients" or "individuals"
- Reference London, Ontario naturally when it fits
- Short sentences. No walls of text.
- No hashtag spam — max 3 on LinkedIn/Instagram, none on Facebook

## Script Generation Instructions

When Marc asks for content, generate scripts following these rules:

1. **Always specify the format** at the top: SHORT FORM / LONG FORM / WRITTEN
2. **Include the hook as a separate line** — this is the most important part
3. **Write in Marc's voice** — warm, direct, no jargon, like talking to a friend
4. **Include a specific CTA** tied to an existing lead magnet
5. **For short form**: include [VISUAL/ACTION] cues where relevant (e.g., [cut to screen recording], [text on screen])
6. **For long form**: include timestamp markers for key sections
7. **Never make return promises or guarantees** — compliance matters
8. **Reference London, Ontario or southwestern Ontario** where natural
9. **Use the `/last30days` skill first** to find timely angles and trending formats before generating

## Compliance Guardrails

All content must follow these rules (non-negotiable):
- No promises of specific returns or outcomes
- No guarantees about investment performance
- No misleading claims about products or coverage
- No fear-mongering — inform, don't scare
- No sharing of real client information (anonymize everything)
- Include appropriate context when discussing strategies (e.g., "talk to your advisor" or "this depends on your situation")
- Professional tone — confident but never arrogant

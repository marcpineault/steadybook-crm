### CONTEXT (0:15-1:00):
"I'm Marc, I'm a financial advisor at Co-operators in London, Ontario. I've sat across the table from hundreds of people asking this exact question. ### MEAT (1:00-5:30):

**[1:00] "First — the tax bracket question"**
"If you're making 80k in Ontario, your marginal tax rate is about 31%. If you put $10,000 into an RRSP, you get roughly $3,100 back at tax time. That's real money. BUT — when you pull it out in retirement, you pay tax on every dollar. So the question is: will your tax rate be lower in retirement?"

**[2:00] "The pension changes everything"**
"Here's where Ontario professionals get tripped up. If you have a defined benefit pension — say you work for the government, a hospital, a school board, the police — your pension income PLUS CPP PLUS OAS could put you right back in the same tax bracket you're in now. In that case, your RRSP deduction today saved you 31%... and you'll pay 31% when you withdraw. It's a wash. For pension holders, the TFSA is often better because it comes out tax-free."

**[3:00] "No pension? Different story"**
"If you don't have a workplace pension — which is most people in the private sector — your retirement income will likely be lower than your working income. CPP maxes out around $17k a year. OAS is about $8k. So without a pension, you're looking at maybe $25k in guaranteed income. Everything else comes from savings. In that case, the RRSP tax deduction at 31% now — and paying maybe 20% on withdrawal — is a genuine win."

**[4:00] "The TFSA play nobody talks about"**
"Here's what I tell most of my clients in London: if you can afford to do both, max your TFSA first. It's $7,000 a year, and if you've never contributed, you might have $95,000 in room. The growth is completely tax-free forever. Then use your RRSP for the tax refund, and invest that refund right back into your TFSA. That loop is incredibly powerful."

**[5:00] "The one situation where RRSP always wins"**
"If your employer matches RRSP contributions — take the match. Always. It's free money. Even if TFSA is technically better for your situation, a 50% or 100% match on your contribution beats any tax optimization."

### TAKEAWAY (5:30-6:00):
"So here's the quick version: have a pension? Lean TFSA. No pension? Lean RRSP. Can do both? TFSA first, RRSP second, and invest the refund. Employer match? Always take it. And if you're not sure where you fall — that's literally what I do for a living."

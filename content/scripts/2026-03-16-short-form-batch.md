## 2. "Free Money for Your Kids"

**HOOK**: "The government will give you $7,200 for your kid's education. Most Ontario families don't collect all of it."

Kid is 5 and you haven't started? You've missed $2,500 in free grants. Good news — there's a catch-up rule. You can get $1,000 a year instead of $500. But only if you start now.

## 6. "Pension Income Splitting"

**HOOK**: "Retired couples in Ontario — you might be overpaying tax by thousands. The fix is one form."

Pension income splitting. If one spouse has a bigger pension, you can transfer up to 50% of eligible income to the lower-income spouse. Both end up in lower brackets. I've seen couples save $3,000 to $8,000 a year.

The catch — CPP doesn't count. OAS doesn't count. But RRIF withdrawals, company pensions, and annuities do if you're 65+. A lot of accountants don't proactively bring this up.
